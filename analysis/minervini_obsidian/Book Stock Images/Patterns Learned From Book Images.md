## What The Images Teach

1. The same logic repeats across the books: leadership first, controlled entry second, sell discipline always.
2. The best buy images usually combine stage-2 behavior, a narrowing range, and a pivot that keeps risk definable.
3. IPO and new-issue images are not automatically bullish. The image must show evidence of demand, tightness, and support after listing.
4. Volume dry-up matters because it shows supply becoming scarce before the entry, not after the move is already obvious.
5. Failure images are part of the system. A clean setup that breaks character becomes a sell or avoid lesson, not a reason to average down.
6. Images without usable tickers still matter when they teach recognition: tightness, shakeout, exhaustion, risk control, or mental mistakes.
