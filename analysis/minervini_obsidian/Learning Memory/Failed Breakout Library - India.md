## Detailed Case Study: RVNL — Failed Breakout → Re-base → Real Breakout Pattern

### The Pattern

RVNL repeatedly shows a specific behavior: the **first breakout fails** (hits hard stop or breakeven), but the stock does not collapse — it **re-consolidates** with **tighter range** and **lower volume**, then produces a **second breakout** that runs massively.

| Case | Failed Entry | Exit | Re-base | Real Entry | Run |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **1** | Jul 21, 2023 @ ₹134.9 | Hard Stop -7.0% | 34 days | **Sep 1, 2023 @ ₹138.3** | **+38.6%** → ₹191.75 |
| **2** | Dec 11, 2023 @ ₹177.4 | Breakeven Stop 0% | 21 days | **Jan 10, 2024 @ ₹197.4** | **+62.3%** → ₹320.35 |

### Key Signals That The Re-base Is Working

1. **Tighter price range** — The base after the failed breakout has a narrower range than the original base (e.g., 7% → 4%)
2. **Volume dry-up** — Trading volume shrinks significantly (supply exhausted): 15-30M → 6-9M shares
3. **Higher lows** — Within the re-base, each pullback finds support at a higher level
4. **Pivot test with volume** — A session that pokes above the prior pivot high with above-average volume (but doesn't break out yet)
5. **Followed by tightening** — 2-4 more days of tight consolidation after the pivot test before the actual move

### What To Learn

**This is NOT a sell-rule study — it is a RE-ENTRY study.**

The Minervini rule says: cut losses quickly and do not argue with the market. This is correct. However, for certain high-momentum stocks (especially Indian PSU/infra names like RVNL, BEL, IRFC, HAL), a failed first breakout is often followed by:

1. A **tighter re-consolidation** (not a collapse)
2. A **secondary breakout** 3-5 weeks later
3. A **massive pole** on the second attempt

The actionable takeaway: **After a failed breakout, do not ignore the stock. Keep tracking the base. Watch for volume drying up and range contracting further. The real breakout often comes on the second attempt.**
