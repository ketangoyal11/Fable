# Entry Planning Playbook From Book Images

## The Entry Is A Test, Not A Prediction

The planned entry tests whether demand is strong enough to overcome supply at a specific price. The trader does not need certainty; the trader needs a tight invalidation point and evidence that the odds are favorable.

## VCP / Contraction Entry

Planning:

- Identify the highest-quality prior advance.
- Wait for pullbacks to become progressively less damaging.
- Prefer the final contraction to sit near the old high or pivot area.
- Look for quieter volume before the move.
- Place the stop where the final contraction has clearly failed.

## Pivot Breakout Entry

Planning:

- Define the pivot before the breakout.
- Avoid buying far above the pivot.
- Require price to move through the pivot with conviction.
- If the breakout reverses quickly, treat that as information.

## Cheat Entry

Planning:

- Use only when the stock is already a clear leader.
- Enter before the obvious pivot only if a smaller tight area appears.
- The stop must be close; otherwise it is just early chasing.
- The cheat entry should reduce risk, not increase impulsiveness.

## IPO / New Issue Entry

Planning:

- Let the stock form a first real structure.
- Do not buy purely because the company is new or popular.
- Watch whether the IPO range holds.
- Prefer tightness, dry-up, and a pivot that forms after early volatility calms.

## Shakeout Entry

Planning:

- A shakeout should recover quickly.
- It should remove weak holders without causing broad technical damage.
- The entry is considered only after price regains the key area.
- If it does not recover, it is not a shakeout; it is failure.

## Failed Breakout / Stage 4 Avoidance

Planning:

- If the pivot fails, stop treating the pattern as a buy.
- Watch for heavy-volume downside reversals.
- A clean base that breaks support becomes a sell-rule case.
- The best loss is the one taken while it is still small.

## Climax / Exhaustion

Planning:

- Look for a stock that has already made a major move.
- Watch for vertical price action, emotional volume, and wide bars.
- This is not an entry pattern; it is a risk-reduction pattern.

## The Practical Buy Checklist

| Gate | Question |
|---|---|
| Leadership | Is the stock acting better than the market and peers? |
| Trend | Is it in stage 2 or moving into stage 2? |
| Structure | Is there a base, shelf, handle, or VCP? |
| Contraction | Are swings getting smaller near the pivot? |
| Volume | Is supply drying up before the move? |
| Pivot | Is the buy point obvious before the trade? |
| Risk | Is the stop close enough for the expected gain? |
| Failure | What exact behavior proves the thesis wrong? |
