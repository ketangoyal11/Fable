# Volatility Contraction Pattern

## Core Idea

A VCP is a base where volatility narrows through successive pullbacks. The visual message is that supply is being absorbed, sellers are becoming less aggressive, and risk can be defined near a clear pivot.

## Source Pages To Review

- [[Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard]]: candidate pages 213, 345, 214, 216, 218, 215, 220, 233, 241, 242
- [[Think and Trade Like a Champion]]: candidate pages 110, 109, 116, 212, 211, 111, 170, 200, 28, 112

## Checklist

- [ ] Strong prior advance exists before the base.
- [ ] At least two contractions are visible.
- [ ] Each contraction is generally smaller or tighter than the prior one.
- [ ] Volume dries up during the final contraction.
- [ ] Pivot is clear and close enough to allow a sensible stop.

## Image Work

- Box the base and label contraction 1, 2, and 3.
- Draw the pivot line across the tight area.
- Mark volume dry-up near the final contraction.

## Scanner Mapping

- `Look for decreasing rolling ATR or shrinking high-low ranges.`
- `Require tight closes near recent highs and reduced volume near the pivot.`
