# Industry Maturation Curve

The Industry Maturation Curve shows how industries evolve from many competitors to a few dominant players, creating opportunities to invest in emerging leaders.

## Examples

- Automobile industry (1890-1970) — Hundreds of manufacturers consolidated to a few
- Television industry (1940-1990) — Same consolidation pattern
- Disk drives (1976-1998) — Technology lifecycle
- Personal computers (1974-1992) — Consolidation winners

## Key Insight

The companies that survive the shakeout and emerge as leaders often become the best investments.
