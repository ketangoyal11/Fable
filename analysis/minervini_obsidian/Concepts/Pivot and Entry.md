# Pivot and Entry

## Core Idea

The entry should occur as close as practical to a valid pivot so the trade has clear invalidation. Buying too far above the pivot turns a good idea into poor risk control.

## Source Pages To Review

- [[Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard]]: candidate pages 244, 238, 218, 239, 242, 245, 253, 264, 265, 241
- [[Think and Trade Like a Champion]]: candidate pages 116, 117, 39, 132, 205, 114, 26, 29, 30, 36

## Checklist

- [ ] Pivot is based on a real area of resistance or tight supply.
- [ ] Breakout has price confirmation and ideally volume expansion.
- [ ] Entry is not extended from the pivot or key moving averages.
- [ ] Stop location is known before entry.
- [ ] Reward-to-risk remains attractive after slippage.

## Image Work

- Draw horizontal pivot, entry marker, stop line, and initial risk box.
