# Lowest Volume Before Breakout

The Lowest Volume Before Breakout is a classic SEPA signal: volume dries up to its lowest point just before the pivot break, indicating supply exhaustion.

> "Note the lowest volume and super tight price action the day before the breakout."

## Why It Matters

When volume is at its lowest and price is tightest, it means:
1. Everyone who wanted to sell has sold
2. No one is left to press the stock lower
3. The next move will likely be explosive
