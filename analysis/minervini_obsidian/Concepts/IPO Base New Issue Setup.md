# IPO Base / New Issue Setup

## Core Idea

An IPO or new issue should be treated as fresh merchandise only after price proves that institutions are accumulating it. The key is not the listing itself; the key is the first constructive structure after the listing.

## What To Look For

- The stock resists broad market weakness after listing.
- A first base or tight area forms without repeated heavy-volume breaks.
- Volume dries up near the final tight section.
- Price clears a definable pivot while risk remains controllable.
- Fundamentals or narrative support new leadership, but price must confirm.

## Failure Clues

- The stock loses the IPO range and cannot reclaim it.
- The first rally becomes too extended before a proper setup forms.
- Heavy selling appears before a stable base develops.
- A breakout quickly reverses and violates the pivot.
