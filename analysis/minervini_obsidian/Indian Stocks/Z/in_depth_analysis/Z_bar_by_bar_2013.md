## Phase 3: Tennis Ball Action (Brief Pullbacks)

The caption says: **"Pullbacks were brief and met with support."**

This is the hallmark of **tennis ball action** — the stock doesn't give back much of its gains before bouncing back. The pullbacks are characterized by:

- **Duration:** Brief (1–3 days typically)
- **Depth:** Shallow (pulling to the 10-day or 20-day moving average)
- **Volume:** Declining during pullbacks (lack of supply)
- **Recovery:** Immediate snapback to new highs (tennis ball behavior)

**Why this matters:** Tennis ball action is evidence of strong institutional accumulation. When institutions are building positions, they buy every dip, creating a "floor" under the stock.
