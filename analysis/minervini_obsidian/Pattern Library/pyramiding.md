## Book Examples

### AAPL — Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard p196

- **Pattern Subtype:** follow-up-buys-in-winner
- **Date Range:** 2003-2004
- **Entry Level:** Initial buy around $11-12 (March 2004), Follow-Up Buy higher, 3rd Buy even higher
- **Stop Level:** Trailing stop below rising 50-day MA
- **Outcome:** More than doubled after purchases
- **Key Lesson:** Pyramid into winners. Initial position at breakout, add on follow-through. Each add should be smaller than the previous. Trail stop with rising MA.
- **Trend Template Score (replicated):** 8/8
- **VCP Quality:** loose
- **20d Max Gain:** 17.35%
- **20d Max DD:** -3.51%
- **Replicated Chart:** [[replicated_charts/trade-like-a-stock-market-wizard-p196-aapl_replicated.png]]

> **Kimi Notes:** Rare insight into Minervinis actual execution. He buys initial position at breakout, then adds on follow-up buys as stock continues higher. This is pyramiding done right - adding to winners, not losers. The 3rd Buy is smaller than initial, preserving risk discipline.
