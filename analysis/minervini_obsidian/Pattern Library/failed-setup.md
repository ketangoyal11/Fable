## Book Examples

### CROX — Think and Trade Like a Champion p90

- **Pattern Subtype:** earnings-rejection-climax-sell
- **Date Range:** 2007-2008
- **Entry Level:** N/A - no valid entry, stock was failing
- **Stop Level:** N/A - sell signal on earnings day
- **Outcome:** Crashed from ~$75 to near zero
- **Key Lesson:** On Nov 1, 2007, CROX reported earnings up 144% but stock closed DOWN 36% on heaviest volume since IPO. Stories and earnings do not move prices - people do. When price action contradicts fundamentals, trust the price action.
- **Trend Template Score (replicated):** 8/8
- **VCP Quality:** constructive
- **20d Max Gain:** 0.0%
- **20d Max DD:** -35.12%
- **Replicated Chart:** [[replicated_charts/think-and-trade-like-a-champion-p090-crox_replicated.png]]

> **Kimi Notes:** One of the most important lessons in the book. CROX had great earnings but the MARKET rejected it violently. This is called an earnings rejection or climax sell. The largest volume since IPO on a down day means institutions are dumping. Never fight this signal.
